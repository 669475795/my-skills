## Four-Quadrant Macro Framework

Map every market environment into one of four quadrants using two dimensions:
- **Growth**: GDP accelerating (↑) or decelerating (↓)
- **Inflation**: CPI/PCE rising (↑) or falling (↓)

```
                    INFLATION ↑
                         │
         Stagflation     │    Overheating
         (Growth↓,Infl↑) │    (Growth↑,Infl↑)
                         │
GROWTH ↓ ────────────────┼──────────────────── GROWTH ↑
                         │
         Deflation/Bust  │    Goldilocks
         (Growth↓,Infl↓) │    (Growth↑,Infl↓)
                         │
                    INFLATION ↓
```

## Key Cross-Asset Relationships

### Gold Relationships

| Relationship | Correlation | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| Gold vs USD | Negative (typically -0.6 to -0.8) | USD up → Gold down (inverse) |
| Gold vs Real Yields | Negative (strong) | Real yield up → Gold opportunity cost rises |
| Gold vs Inflation | Positive (long-term) | Inflation hedge, but short-term complex |
| Gold vs Geopolitical risk | Positive | Crisis hedge demand |
| Gold vs BTC | Loosely positive | Both "stores of value" but BTC more volatile |

**Gold buy conditions:**
- Real yields falling or negative
- USD weakening
- Geopolitical escalation
- Central bank buying (China, India, EM)
- MVRV-type metrics for gold: Gold/Inflation-adjusted price relative to history

---

### Equities vs Bonds

| Signal | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| Stock/Bond correlation negative (normal) | Bonds are portfolio hedge |
| Stock/Bond correlation positive (crisis) | Both sell off together, no hedge |
| Yield curve inversion (2Y > 10Y) | Recession signal, watch 6-18 months out |
| Yield curve steepening from inversion | Early cycle recovery signal |
| Credit spreads widening | Risk-off, stress in corporate sector |
| Credit spreads tightening | Risk appetite improving |

## Interest Rate Regime Analysis

### Fed Policy Cycle and Asset Impact

```
RATE CUTTING CYCLE                    RATE HIKING CYCLE
├── Bonds: ★★★★★ (price rises)       ├── Bonds: ★★ (price falls)
├── REITs: ★★★★★ (discount rate ↓)  ├── REITs: ★★ (cap rate compress)
├── Utilities: ★★★★ (yield premium)  ├── Utilities: ★★ (yield competition)
├── Banks: ★★ (NIM compresses)       ├── Banks: ★★★★ (NIM expands)
├── Gold: ★★★★ (real yields fall)    ├── Gold: ★★ (real yields rise)
├── Tech growth: ★★★★ (DCF benefit)  ├── Tech growth: ★★ (high duration)
└── USD: ★★ (weakens)               └── USD: ★★★★ (strengthens)
```

### Yield Curve Signals

| Curve Shape | Meaning | Historical Outcome |
|------------|---------|-------------------|
| Normal (steep: 10Y > 2Y) | Growth expectations | Bull market conditions |
| Flat (10Y ≈ 2Y) | Late cycle | Slow growth ahead |
| Inverted (2Y > 10Y) | Recession warning | Recession in 6-18 months (historically) |
| Bull steepening (rates falling) | Recovery | Risk assets rally |
| Bear steepening (long rates rise faster) | Inflation fears | Mixed; cyclicals may still work |

## USD Strength Analysis

### DXY (Dollar Index) Drivers

| Factor | USD Impact |
|--------|-----------|
| US rates higher than peers | USD strengthens |
| Fed hawkish vs other central banks | USD strengthens |
| US growth outperformance | USD strengthens |
| Risk-off / flight to safety | USD strengthens |
| US fiscal deficit expanding | USD weakens long-term |
| Current account deficit | USD weakens |
| EM growth acceleration | USD weakens |

### USD Strength Impact by Asset

**Strong USD (DXY rising)**:
- US multinationals: Headwind (foreign earnings translate back lower)
- Commodities: Headwind (priced in USD, foreign buyers pay more)
- EM equities/bonds: Headwind (capital outflows, USD-denominated debt)
- Gold: Headwind (historical inverse relationship)
- US domestic-focused stocks: Neutral to positive

**Weak USD (DXY falling)**:
- All of the above reversed
- EM: Significant tailwind
- Gold: Significant tailwind
- Commodities: Tailwind

## Risk-On / Risk-Off Framework

### Risk-On Environment
*Capital moves toward higher-risk, higher-return assets*

**Signals:**
- VIX < 15
- Credit spreads (HY-IG) tightening
- EM currencies strengthening vs USD
- Small caps outperforming large caps
- Cyclicals outperforming defensives
- Bitcoin rising relative to gold

**Asset rotation:**
- Sell: Bonds, Gold, Yen, Swiss Franc, Utilities
- Buy: Equities, Commodities, EM, High Yield bonds, Crypto

---

### Risk-Off Environment
*Capital moves toward safety and liquidity*

**Signals:**
- VIX > 25 (spike)
- Credit spreads widening rapidly
- EM currencies weakening vs USD
- Defensive sectors outperforming
- Gold rising, Bitcoin falling (crypto sells off with risk assets)
- Flight to Treasuries (yields fall)

**Asset rotation:**
- Sell: Equities, EM, High Yield, Commodities, Crypto
- Buy: Treasuries, Gold, Yen, Swiss Franc, USD, Utilities


### Step 2: Determine Quadrant
Answer these two questions:
1. Is GDP growth accelerating or decelerating? (check: PMI trend, GDP revisions, yield curve)
2. Is inflation rising or falling? (check: CPI MoM, Core PCE, inflation expectations)

### Step 3: Check Risk Appetite
```bash
python scripts/sentiment_analysis.py vix
python scripts/fetch_stock_data.py SPY HYG GLD UUP TLT --period 1mo
```

- DXY direction (rising = USD strength)

- Credit spreads (IG ETF: LQD, HY ETF: HYG) direction
