# Investment Analysis Frameworks

## Fundamental Analysis Framework

### 1. Company Analysis (Stocks)

#### Financial Health
- **Profitability**: Net profit margin, ROE, ROA
- **Liquidity**: Current ratio, quick ratio
- **Solvency**: Debt-to-equity ratio, interest coverage
- **Efficiency**: Asset turnover, inventory turnover

#### Valuation Metrics
- **P/E Ratio**: Price relative to earnings
  - < 15: Potentially undervalued
  - 15-25: Fair value
  - > 25: Potentially overvalued (or growth stock)
- **P/B Ratio**: Price to book value
  - < 1: Potentially undervalued
  - > 3: Premium valuation
- **PEG Ratio**: P/E adjusted for growth
  - < 1: Undervalued relative to growth
  - > 2: Overvalued relative to growth
- **Dividend Yield**: Annual dividends / Price

#### Growth Indicators
- Revenue growth (YoY)
- Earnings growth (YoY)
- Market share trends
- Product pipeline

---

### 2. Cryptocurrency Analysis

#### On-Chain Metrics
- **Market Cap**: Total value of all coins
- **Circulating Supply**: Available coins
- **Trading Volume**: Liquidity indicator
- **Active Addresses**: Network activity
- **Hash Rate** (Bitcoin): Network security

#### Fundamental Factors
- **Use Case**: Utility and adoption potential
- **Technology**: Innovation and scalability
- **Team**: Developer activity and credibility
- **Community**: Social engagement and support
- **Tokenomics**: Supply model, inflation rate
- **Partnerships**: Real-world adoption

#### Valuation Approaches
- **Network Value to Transactions (NVT)**: Market cap / Transaction volume
- **MVRV Ratio**: Market value / Realized value
- **Stock-to-Flow**: Scarcity model (Bitcoin)

## Technical Analysis Framework

### Trend Analysis Process

1. **Identify Primary Trend** (Long-term)
   - Use SMA(200) on daily chart
   - Trend direction: Up/Down/Sideways

2. **Identify Secondary Trend** (Medium-term)
   - Use SMA(50) on daily chart
   - Relationship to primary trend

3. **Identify Short-term Patterns**
   - Chart patterns: Triangles, head & shoulders, etc.
   - Support and resistance levels

4. **Entry Timing**
   - Use RSI, MACD for entry signals
   - Wait for pullbacks in uptrends
   - Enter on breakouts with volume confirmation

---

### Multi-Timeframe Analysis

| Timeframe | Purpose | Indicators |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Monthly/Weekly | Primary trend | SMA(200), Major S/R levels |
| Daily | Entry/exit timing | MACD, RSI, Bollinger Bands |
| 4H/1H | Fine-tune entries | EMA(20), Volume |

**Process**:
1. Monthly: Determine overall trend (bullish/bearish/neutral)
2. Weekly: Identify key support/resistance zones
3. Daily: Wait for setup (indicator signals)
4. 4H/1H: Execute entry with tight stop-loss

## Risk Management Framework

### Position Sizing

**Fixed Percentage Method**:
```
Position Size = (Account Size × Risk%) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)
```

**Example**:
- Account: $10,000
- Risk per trade: 2% ($200)
- Entry: $100
- Stop Loss: $95
- Position Size: $200 / $5 = 40 shares

**Guidelines**:
- Risk 1-2% per trade (conservative)
- Risk 2-5% per trade (moderate)
- Never risk >10% per trade

---

### Stop Loss Strategies

1. **Fixed Percentage**: -5% from entry
2. **ATR-based**: Entry ± (2 × ATR)
3. **Technical Level**: Below support (long) or above resistance (short)
4. **Trailing Stop**: Follows price, locks in profits

**Example**:
- Long entry: $100
- ATR: $3
- Stop Loss: $100 - (2 × $3) = $94

---

### Risk-Reward Ratio

**Minimum**: 1:2 (Risk $1 to make $2)
**Ideal**: 1:3 or better

**Calculation**:
```
Risk = Entry Price - Stop Loss
Reward = Take Profit - Entry Price
Ratio = Reward / Risk
```

**Example**:
- Entry: $100
- Stop Loss: $95 (Risk: $5)
- Target: $115 (Reward: $15)
- Ratio: 15/5 = 1:3 ✓

## Portfolio Construction

### Diversification Strategy

#### Asset Allocation
- **Conservative**: 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% alternatives
- **Moderate**: 70% stocks, 20% bonds, 10% alternatives
- **Aggressive**: 85% stocks, 10% alternatives, 5% cash

#### Geographic Diversification
- US Market: 40-50%
- International Developed: 20-30%
- Emerging Markets: 10-20%
- Alternative Assets: 10-20%

#### Sector Diversification (Stocks)
- Technology: 15-20%
- Healthcare: 10-15%
- Financials: 10-15%
- Consumer: 10-15%
- Industrials: 10-15%
- Energy: 5-10%
- Others: 15-20%

#### Crypto Portfolio
- Bitcoin (BTC): 50-70% (store of value)
- Ethereum (ETH): 20-30% (smart contracts)
- Altcoins: 10-20% (growth/speculation)

---

### Rebalancing

**Frequency**: Quarterly or when allocation drifts >5%

**Example**:
- Target: 60% stocks, 40% bonds
- Current: 65% stocks, 35% bonds
- Action: Sell 5% stocks, buy 5% bonds

## Investment Decision Framework

### Buy Decision Checklist

#### Technical Criteria (3 of 4 required)
- [ ] Price above SMA(50) and SMA(200)
- [ ] RSI between 30-70 (not extreme)
- [ ] MACD bullish crossover
- [ ] Volume confirmation on breakout

#### Fundamental Criteria (Stocks: 3 of 5 required)
- [ ] P/E ratio below industry average or < 20
- [ ] Revenue growth > 10% YoY
- [ ] Positive earnings
- [ ] Debt-to-equity ratio < 1.5
- [ ] Analyst consensus: Buy/Outperform

#### Fundamental Criteria (Crypto: 2 of 4 required)
- [ ] Strong development activity
- [ ] Growing active addresses
- [ ] Clear use case and adoption
- [ ] Favorable NVT or MVRV ratio

#### Risk Assessment (All required)
- [ ] Risk-reward ratio ≥ 1:2
- [ ] Position size within risk limits (≤2% of portfolio)
- [ ] Stop loss defined
- [ ] Exit strategy planned

---

### Sell Decision Triggers

**Profit Taking**:
- Target price reached
- Risk-reward ratio no longer favorable
- Technical indicators showing weakness

**Stop Loss**:
- Price hits stop loss level
- Break of key support level
- Fundamental deterioration

**Rebalancing**:
- Position becomes too large (>10% of portfolio)
- Need to redeploy to better opportunities

## Market Sentiment Analysis

### Fear & Greed Indicators

**Bull Market Signs**:
- VIX (Volatility Index) < 15
- High trading volume
- New 52-week highs increasing
- Positive news flow
- Retail investor enthusiasm

**Bear Market Signs**:
- VIX > 30
- Declining volume
- New 52-week lows increasing
- Negative headlines
- Panic selling

---

### Contrarian Indicators

**Excessive Optimism** (Potential top):
- Magazine covers about getting rich in stocks
- Everyone talking about investments
- Extreme valuations (P/E ratios >30)
- Low volatility (complacency)

**Excessive Pessimism** (Potential bottom):
- Capitulation selling
- High volatility (VIX >40)
- Extreme negative sentiment
- "This time is different" bearishness

## Investment Styles

### Value Investing
**Approach**: Buy undervalued companies
**Key Metrics**: P/E, P/B, Dividend yield
**Holding Period**: Long-term (years)
**Risk**: Lower volatility
**Example**: Warren Buffett

### Growth Investing
**Approach**: Buy fast-growing companies
**Key Metrics**: Revenue growth, earnings growth, PEG
**Holding Period**: Medium to long-term
**Risk**: Higher volatility
**Example**: Cathie Wood (ARK)

### Momentum Investing
**Approach**: Buy trending stocks
**Key Metrics**: Price momentum, relative strength
**Holding Period**: Short to medium-term
**Risk**: High volatility
**Example**: Trend followers

### Swing Trading
**Approach**: Capture short-term moves
**Key Metrics**: Technical indicators
**Holding Period**: Days to weeks
**Risk**: Moderate to high
**Requirements**: Active monitoring

### Day Trading
**Approach**: Intraday price movements
**Key Metrics**: Volume, technical patterns
**Holding Period**: Minutes to hours
**Risk**: Very high
**Requirements**: Full-time commitment, experience

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**: Chasing pumps
2. **Revenge Trading**: Trying to recover losses quickly
3. **Overtrading**: Too many positions, high fees
4. **Ignoring Stop Losses**: Hoping losses will reverse
5. **Confirmation Bias**: Only seeking supporting information
6. **Averaging Down**: Adding to losing positions without reason
7. **Emotional Trading**: Making decisions based on fear/greed
8. **Lack of Plan**: Trading without clear strategy
9. **Overleveraging**: Using excessive borrowed money
10. **Ignoring Risk Management**: Not sizing positions properly
